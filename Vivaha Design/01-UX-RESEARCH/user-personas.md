# User Personas

> Understanding the people Vivaha is designed for.

---

# Purpose

Vivaha is not designed for "users."

It is designed for real people with different goals, emotions, responsibilities, and expectations.

These personas guide frontend decisions.

They influence

• Layouts

• Navigation

• Typography

• Copywriting

• Accessibility

• Information hierarchy

• Components

• Motion

Whenever a new screen is designed, it should answer:

> Which persona is this screen primarily serving?

---

# Primary Persona 01

# The Groom

Age

24–34

Goals

Find a compatible life partner.

Present himself honestly.

Discover meaningful profiles.

Build genuine conversations.

Pain Points

Large numbers of fake profiles.

Poor profile quality.

Time-consuming browsing.

Lack of transparency.

Overwhelming interfaces.

Needs

Fast profile discovery.

Trustworthy information.

Modern interface.

Simple communication.

Respectful experience.

Frontend Priorities

Clean profile cards.

Fast search.

Minimal distractions.

Readable typography.

Strong filtering.

Simple navigation.

---

# Primary Persona 02

# The Bride

Age

22–32

Goals

Find someone compatible.

Feel safe while browsing.

Maintain privacy.

Understand family values.

Pain Points

Privacy concerns.

Unwanted messages.

Low-quality profiles.

Trust issues.

Information overload.

Needs

Verification.

Privacy controls.

Thoughtful profile presentation.

Comfortable conversations.

Professional environment.

Frontend Priorities

Clear privacy settings.

Elegant profile layouts.

Visible verification.

Minimal cognitive load.

Friendly copywriting.

---

# Primary Persona 03

# Parents

Age

45–65

Goals

Help their children find suitable matches.

Evaluate families.

Understand values.

Trust the platform.

Pain Points

Complex interfaces.

Tiny typography.

Too much animation.

Unclear navigation.

Modern UI patterns.

Needs

Large readable text.

Simple navigation.

High trust.

Traditional clarity.

Predictable interactions.

Frontend Priorities

Readable typography.

Straightforward layouts.

Clear labels.

Large touch targets.

Minimal animations.

Excellent accessibility.

---

# Secondary Persona

# The Family

Families often browse together.

Multiple people discuss profiles.

Screens may be viewed together on larger displays.

The interface should never become awkward in shared environments.

Avoid

Flashy messaging.

Dating terminology.

Overly romantic language.

Aggressive visuals.

Support

Shared decision making.

Respect.

Values.

Conversation.

Trust.

---

---

---

## Profile

Serves all personas.

Prioritize readability and authenticity.

---

Calm.

Private.

Minimal.

Distraction free.

---

# Accessibility Considerations

Parents often have

Reduced vision.

Lower digital familiarity.

Less patience for complexity.

Design should therefore include

Larger typography.

Simple language.

Predictable navigation.

Comfortable spacing.

Clear focus states.

Accessibility benefits every persona.

---

# AI Implementation Rules

Before generating a frontend,

identify the primary persona.

Optimize layouts for that persona's needs.

Never create interfaces that only appeal to young, highly technical users.

Every screen should remain welcoming to families.

---

# Review Checklist

□ Who is the primary persona?

□ Does this layout serve their goals?

□ Does it reduce their anxiety?

□ Is the interface readable?

□ Would parents understand it?

□ Does it strengthen trust?

□ Is unnecessary complexity removed?

If any answer is "No",

the screen requires refinement.
