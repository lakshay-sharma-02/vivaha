# Hero Component

> The Hero is the front door of Vivah. It is the first conversation with the user.

---

# Purpose

The Hero establishes emotional trust.

It is not a marketing banner.

It is not an advertisement.

It is not a slideshow.

Its only responsibility is to help visitors feel welcome, understood, and confident enough to begin their journey.

The Hero should answer one question.

"Can I trust Vivah?"

---

# Philosophy

Imagine someone walking into a beautifully designed modern Indian home.

The entrance is clean.

Natural light fills the room.

Fresh flowers sit nearby.

Nothing shouts for attention.

Everything quietly communicates care.

That is exactly how the Hero should feel.

---

# Emotional Objective

The Hero should create

Calm

↓

Trust

↓

Hope

↓

Action

Never

Excitement

Urgency

Fear

Pressure

Luxury

---

# Component Structure

Every Hero follows the same architecture.

```
Atmosphere

↓

Navigation

↓

Headline

↓

Supporting Copy

↓

Primary CTA

↓

Secondary CTA

↓

Supporting Trust Indicators

↓

Visual Environment
```

Nothing else belongs in the Hero.

---

# Primary Message

The Hero introduces Vivah.

Not features.

Not technology.

Not AI.

The message is about people.

Examples

Meaningful relationships begin with trust.

Where families and individuals discover lasting connections.

A thoughtful beginning to one of life's most meaningful journeys.

---

# Supporting Copy

The supporting paragraph should answer

Why Vivah exists.

The tone should remain

Warm.

Professional.

Respectful.

Calm.

Maximum

2–3 lines.

---

# Primary CTA

Only one primary action.

Examples

Create Your Profile

Begin Your Journey

Find Meaningful Connections

Never use

Get Started Now!

Join Before It's Too Late!

Claim Your Match!

---

# Secondary CTA

Lower commitment.

Examples

Learn How Vivah Works

Explore Success Stories

Understand Our Process

The secondary CTA should educate.

Not compete.

---

# Trust Indicators

Placed beneath the CTAs.

Examples

Verified Profiles

Privacy First

Family Friendly

Secure Communication

Thoughtful Matchmaking

Maximum

3–5 indicators.

Simple.

Minimal.

No statistics unless verified.

---

# Hero Visual

The Hero image is not an advertisement.

It should communicate atmosphere.

Preferred imagery

Modern Indian home.

Natural daylight.

White flowers.

Botanical details.

Stone textures.

Warm interiors.

Family conversation.

Peaceful garden.

Reading together.

Quiet companionship.

Avoid

Wedding stages.

Fashion shoots.

Luxury cars.

Heavy jewellery.

Artificial romance.

Stock-photo smiles.

---

# Layout

Desktop

Split composition.

Content on one side.

Visual environment on the other.

Mobile

Single column.

Content first.

Visual second.

Never reverse the reading order.

---

# Content Width

Headline

Maximum 12 words.

Supporting text

Maximum 2–3 lines.

CTAs

Maximum two.

Never overload the Hero.

---

# Visual Hierarchy

Users should notice

Headline

↓

Primary CTA

↓

Supporting Copy

↓

Visual

↓

Trust Indicators

Nothing should compete with the headline.

---

# Background

The Hero background should feel architectural.

Soft ivory.

Natural gradients.

Gentle botanical framing.

Morning light.

Never dark.

Never dramatic.

Never noisy.

---

# Motion

Motion should remain environmental.

Allowed

Soft fade.

Light movement.

Gentle botanical motion.

Subtle image reveal.

Tiny hover feedback.

Forbidden

Carousels.

Auto-playing videos.

Parallax.

Particle storms.

Typing animations.

Countdowns.

Rotating headlines.

---

# Accessibility

Every Hero must support

Keyboard navigation.

Visible focus.

Readable typography.

Accessible contrast.

Screen readers.

CTA labels should clearly describe the action.

---

# Performance

Hero imagery should use

Responsive images.

Modern formats.

Lazy loading below the fold only.

Above-the-fold assets should load immediately.

Target

Largest Contentful Paint under 2.5 seconds.

---

# Responsive Behaviour

Desktop

Immersive.

Tablet

Balanced.

Mobile

Focused.

Never remove essential content.

Only simplify layout.

---

# Frontend Implementation

Recommended structure

```
Hero/

Hero.tsx

HeroContent.tsx

HeroVisual.tsx

HeroCTA.tsx

HeroTrust.tsx

HeroBackground.tsx

index.ts
```

The Hero should consume

Typography Tokens

Spacing Tokens

Color Tokens

Motion Tokens

Surface Tokens

Never define local styles.

---

# AI Implementation Rules

When generating a Hero

Always include

Headline

Supporting copy

Primary CTA

Secondary CTA

Trust indicators

Atmospheric visual

Never include

Carousels.

Marketing counters.

Countdown timers.

Feature grids.

Multiple CTAs.

Autoplay media.

Do not invent new layouts.

Follow the Hero architecture exactly.

---

# Review Checklist

□ Does the Hero establish trust?

□ Is there one clear message?

□ Is there one primary action?

□ Is the layout calm?

□ Is the imagery authentic?

□ Is the typography readable?

□ Is the motion subtle?

□ Does the Hero feel like entering a welcoming home?

Only if every answer is "Yes" should the Hero be approved.
